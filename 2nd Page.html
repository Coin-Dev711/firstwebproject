Super Smash Bros. Ultimate

Created by Masahiro Sakurai



Super Smash Bros. Ultimate had a unique development cycle to say the least, it was a surprise to everyone, even the creator of the game, yes, you read that correctly. If you don't know, Masahiro Sakurai was able to make Super Smash Bros, the first game of the series by getting funding from Nintendo, one of the main reasons being that that was before concepts like self publishing had been popularized and while that was a good idea then, it was the same reason that a 5th installment of Super Smash Bros was being made as Nintendo was not about to let go of one of their most profitable Iconic Franchises, so they decided to start a new game and since they owned the I.P, even if Masahiro Sakurai didn't want to, if he wanted to keep the reputation of one of his most successful franchises, just like Sisyphus and others that came before him, he would have to push the boulder another time. But that wasn't all as that boulder had extra weights this time, as Masahiro Sakurai included every single character that had ever been in a Smash Bros game and more, all in 2.5 years, for reference, Pokemon, a franchise that has been criticized time and time again for having short dev times that leads to releasing games in such a low quality, they must have escaped, takes at least 3 years to make a mainline game, not to mention, Smash Bros isn't as popular as Pokemon, it needs to be polished to fly off shelves and, yet, with all those factors against, Masahiro Sakurai and his team, said "Nah, I'd win" (Surprising if you get that reference) and released one of the most successful games on the switch, a console that has over 150 million units, over 20% of those consoles have Super Smash Bros Ultimate, in other words, the game sold... over 36 million copies. One of the greatest feats in game dev of all time.



Hollow Knight: Silksong

Created by Ari Gibson, William Pellen and Jack Vine

Hollow Knight: Silksong, a game that was so long in development, that most people couldn't tell whether the fans became deranged from the wait or if they were always like that, a game in development for so long, is was proclaimed as the GTA 6 of indie games, a game with so much hype behind it, that game devs changed their release date because they didn't want to be crushed by the Silksong Avalanche, a game so popular, that it crashed the servers of the 3, most popular platforms, Playstation, Xbox and Nintendo. I'm sure you're tired of the intro by now, so let's get to the point, Hollow Knight: Silksong was originally planned to be a DLC, but the devs just continued adding things to it, increasing the scope, delaying, rinse and repeat, until they decided to make it a full game. Since they were so "locked in", the only "acceptable" excuse I can give for their lack of information, we don't really know much about the development, it presumably went smoothly, since as an indie dev, they had no producer to pressure them and the fans weren't asking them for news, just going wild in their circles. They were able to focus on making the game, so, as far as Hollow Knight was concerned, for the most part, peace and harmony was achieved, at least, up until the... announcement. Team Cherry decided to drop a bomb, on the level of destruction of missiles, they randomly tweeted the release date, September 4th, 2025, a date was set in stone, as it got closer, the tensions grew higher, like a volcano about to erupt, and then... the day came, the game was released, for $20, I might add and sealed itself as one of the greatest games of all time.
